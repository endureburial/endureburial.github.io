Wagon Wheel

Mother. She feeds me. Mother feeds me the things that grow inside her. They trickle like treacle and taste like soft meat. They hang off her skin, gathering weight at the bottom and I pluck them when they’re ripe. Pop them into my mouth and burst the peel with my tooth. I swallow before I chew them up completely. I always swallow before they stop moving.

After my feeding I lie on my back, bloated and half-conscious, an almost post-coital glow radiating from my cheeks. My throat is quenched, my belly full. I am saturated with the thick blood of Mother’s fruit. They squirm in my stomach; I hear small mousy squeals as they dissolve inside me. Their cries make my ears tingle.
