Viennese Whirl

I like you when you’re sick. I love you when you’re weak. My daydreams are often visited by a vision of you in cold sweats, clutching your stomach in quiet desperation. Just the thought of you brings a smile to my face. And, soon enough, I am smiling at the thought of you writhing in green anguish, spluttering crimson drops onto the bedsheet, your pallor growing yellower by the minute. Green, red, gold: it’s like Christmas when you’re poorly.

Grunt my name in the fugue state of influenza. Grasp my hand in the dizzying throes of gastroenteritis. You’re glowing, darling. I cannot put into words the adoration I have of each spasm, each muffled cry through clenched teeth that overcomes your body when you are ill.
